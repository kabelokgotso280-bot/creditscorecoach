Tips & Guides

Full checklist to improve your credit score

  1. Order full credit reports from Experian and TransUnion and review every entry.
  2. Identify incorrect personal info, duplicate accounts, wrong balances, or fraudulent accounts.
  3. Use our dispute templates to submit disputes — include proof and keep copies.
  4. Pay down high utilization accounts first to lower your utilisation ratio.
  5. Set up payment reminders or debit orders to avoid missed payments.
  6. Avoid closing old accounts — length of credit history helps your score.
  7. If you need credit, consider a credit-builder product that reports on-time payments.
  8. Stagger applications for new credit; too many hard queries close together lowers the score.

Dos & Don'ts

Do

Document everything, keep copies of disputes, and follow up — persistence matters.

Don't

Don't pay a company that asks you to create fake accounts or stop paying creditors — those are harmful and illegal.

Do

Negotiate with creditors for affordable payment plans and request a settlement in writing.

Don't

Don't ignore serious collections — address them proactively.

Important: In-house car finance & Rent-to-Buy — what to watch out for

In-house car finance is when the dealer lends to you directly instead of a bank. It can be tempting but comes with risks:

  • Higher interest rates: Dealers often charge much higher APRs than banks.
  • Hidden fees: Admin fees, markups, and packed insurance can increase total cost.
  • Poor reporting: Some in-house deals don't report positive payments to credit bureaus, so they may not help your credit history.
  • Repossession risk: Defaulting can lead to quick repossession and large negative entries on your report.

Rent-to-Buy (or Rent-to-Own) often advertises low monthly payments but can be worse long-term:

  • Expensive over time: Many rent-to-buy contracts have fees and high overall cost compared to buying via a loan.
  • No ownership until fully paid: If you miss payments you can lose the item and past payments may not count toward ownership.
  • Limited credit benefit: These agreements may not be reported to credit bureaus, so they don't help build credit.
  • Complex contracts: Contracts can include clauses that allow the company to raise fees or repossess quickly.

Our recommendation: If possible, get finance via a bank or regulated lender that reports to Experian/TransUnion, compare APRs, and read the full contract. Use rent-to-buy only as last resort and understand the full cost.
