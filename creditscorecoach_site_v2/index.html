How to grow your credit score fast — practical steps

Follow these proven steps to see improvements quickly. Results vary by case, but many clients see measurable uplift within 30–90 days.

1. Check & prioritise errors

Order your report from Experian and TransUnion. Flag incorrect accounts, duplicate listings, or wrong balances — these are often the biggest lifts.

2. Dispute the high-impact items

Start with accounts that carry large balances, wrong defaults, or fraudulent charges. Use our dispute templates for Experian & TransUnion.

3. Reduce utilisation

Lower your credit card balances to under 30% of limits (ideally under 10%). That often improves scores quickly.

4. Fix payment history

Bring accounts current where possible and ask creditors for a 'goodwill' adjustment after a period of on-time payments.

5. Add positive trade lines

Use small, manageable instalments or credit-builder products that report to credit bureaus to add positive history.

6. Avoid new hard enquiries

Limit new loan or credit applications while you're fixing issues — each hard query can lower your score.
